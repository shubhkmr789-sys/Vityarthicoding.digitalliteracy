Task 4: Social Media Do's and Don'ts checklist.

The Do’s (Best Practices)
* Do set your personal accounts to private if you use them strictly for family and friends.
* use professional platforms like LinkedIn and GitHub to showcase your technical projects and certifications.
* fact-check any news or technical "hacks" before sharing them to prevent the spread of misinformation.
* Do engage politely in technical discussions on platforms like Kaggle or Stack Overflow.
* Do use a professional-looking profile picture and a clear bio that mentions your branch and year.

The Don’ts (Common Mistakes)
* Don’t post any sensitive information, such as your OTPs, home address, or private photos.
* engage in "online arguments" or use unprofessional language that could be traced back to your name.
* Don’t share "cracked" software links or unauthorized exam materials, as this violates academic ethics.
* Don’t tag professors or college officials in casual or irrelevant personal posts.
* post anything you wouldn't be comfortable showing to a future employer during an interview.
